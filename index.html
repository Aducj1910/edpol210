Undocumented Students & Barriers to College

From Marginalization...

...to Resilience

Introduction

  • Nearly 100,000 undocumented students graduate from high school in the United States annually.
  • Undocumented students face numerous legal and social challenges, hindering their ability to thrive in higher academic environments.
  • These challenges also have a profound impact on their mental well-being.

Current Landscape

Nearly 100,000 undocumented students graduate US high school every year.
Migration Policy Institute
  • Apart from the new graduates, there are already about 408,000 undocumented students in US colleges.
  • DACA provided protections to Dreamers but it has since been paused under the Trump administration.
  • Majority of the US states also don't provide any financial aid to undocumented students.

Data from the Migration Policy Institute showing the number of undocumented high school graduates in US states.

Barriers to Higher Education

Financial Barriers

  • Undocumented students often come from low-income Latino communities.
  • Lack access to financial aid and in-state tuition in many states.
  • College becomes unaffordable, limiting upward mobility.

Social Barriers

  • Experience social exclusion, stigma, and fear of deportation.
  • Discrimination and financial struggles restrict campus participation.
  • Impacts mental health and academic performance.

Legal Barriers

  • Non-DACA students face limited professional opportunities and deportation fears.
  • Risk of being unable to return after studying abroad, as shared by one student.

Resilience in Action
